## Scene 2 — The Plaintiff Lays It Out

He is ordinary in the best possible way — button-down shirt, sensible shoes, the kind of guy whose idea of chaos is parking in the wrong zone. But his eyes carry the thousand-yard stare of someone who has been explaining the concept of "private property" to houseguests for seven consecutive months. He smooths a stack of handwritten notes that are, on closer inspection, just a single index card that reads "MY HOUSE, MY RULES" in capital letters. He explains, methodically and without raising his voice, that his brother-in-law moved in after a divorce-related housing collapse and then, two months later, imported his two adult daughters like bonus roommates nobody ordered.
