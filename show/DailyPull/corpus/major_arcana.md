# Major Arcana — the 22 trumps of the Rider–Waite–Smith tarot

The card meanings and imagery below derive from A. E. Waite's *The Pictorial Key
to the Tarot* (1911) and the Rider–Waite–Smith deck (1909, illustrated by Pamela
Colman Smith) — **public domain**. Each block is one card, parsed by
`compute.js`: the `## <number> — <Name>` header is the cast/leading-character
name, and the **Keywords / Upright / Reversed / Persona** fields feed the daily
dramatization. The `Persona` line is the personification brief the artwork and
screenplay lean on (the campaign's art-nouveau style is appended on top).

## 0 — The Fool
**Keywords:** beginnings, innocence, spontaneity, a leap of faith
**Upright:** A fresh start, a leap into the unknown, trusting the road before you can see it.
**Reversed:** Recklessness, a leap taken blind, ignoring the cliff edge that everyone else can see.
**Persona:** The Fool — a bright-eyed young wanderer in motley with a knapsack on a stick and a small white dog at the heel, stepping cheerfully toward a cliff edge under a white sun, weightless and unafraid.

## 1 — The Magician
**Keywords:** manifestation, will, skill, focused power
**Upright:** You already hold every tool you need; channel the will and make it real.
**Reversed:** Talent squandered, manipulation, power pointed at the wrong target.
**Persona:** The Magician — a poised figure in white and red robes, one hand raised to the sky and one to the earth, an infinity sign above the brow, the four suit-symbols laid on the table, roses and lilies blooming around.

## 2 — The High Priestess
**Keywords:** intuition, mystery, the subconscious, inner knowing
**Upright:** The answer is felt before it is reasoned; trust the quiet voice and the things unsaid.
**Reversed:** Secrets kept too long, intuition ignored, a veil that hides rather than protects.
**Persona:** The High Priestess — a serene veiled woman seated between a black pillar and a white pillar, a crescent moon at her feet and a scroll of hidden law on her lap, calm, luminous and unreadable.

## 3 — The Empress
**Keywords:** abundance, nurturing, creation, the senses
**Upright:** Growth, fertility, comfort — something you have tended is ripening; receive it.
**Reversed:** Smothering, creative block, neglecting your own needs to feed everyone else.
**Persona:** The Empress — a radiant crowned mother on a cushioned throne in a field of golden wheat, a heart-shaped shield at her side, a forest and waterfall behind, generous and warm.

## 4 — The Emperor
**Keywords:** authority, structure, control, the father
**Upright:** Order out of chaos; set the boundary, build the frame, lead from steadiness.
**Reversed:** Rigidity, domination, control that has curdled into tyranny.
**Persona:** The Emperor — a stern bearded ruler on a stone throne carved with rams' heads, armour beneath the robe, an ankh sceptre in hand, barren red mountains behind, immovable.

## 5 — The Hierophant
**Keywords:** tradition, teaching, belief, institutions
**Upright:** Wisdom passed down; the proven path, a mentor, the value of the established way.
**Reversed:** Dogma, hollow ritual, breaking from convention to find your own creed.
**Persona:** The Hierophant — a robed high priest between two stone pillars raising a hand in blessing, a triple crown and crossed keys at his feet, two acolytes kneeling before him.

## 6 — The Lovers
**Keywords:** union, choice, alignment, values
**Upright:** A meaningful union or a choice of the heart; choose what aligns with your deepest values.
**Reversed:** Disharmony, misalignment, a choice made for the wrong reasons.
**Persona:** The Lovers — a man and a woman beneath a radiant winged angel, a tree of flame behind one and a fruited tree behind the other, a mountain rising between them, sunlit and open.

## 7 — The Chariot
**Keywords:** willpower, drive, victory through control
**Upright:** Hold the reins of opposing forces and drive forward; victory by sheer focused will.
**Reversed:** Loss of control, scattered direction, forcing a path that won't be forced.
**Persona:** The Chariot — an armoured charioteer crowned with stars under a starry canopy, a black sphinx and a white sphinx yoked before the car, a walled city behind, driven and resolute.

## 8 — Strength
**Keywords:** courage, patience, gentle power, inner mastery
**Upright:** Quiet courage tames the wild thing; soft strength outlasts brute force.
**Reversed:** Self-doubt, raw force without patience, a temper unmastered.
**Persona:** Strength — a calm woman in a white gown, a garland of flowers about her, gently closing the jaws of a great tawny lion with bare hands, an infinity sign over her head, serene and unafraid.

## 9 — The Hermit
**Keywords:** introspection, solitude, inner guidance, the lantern
**Upright:** Withdraw to hear yourself; the light you seek is the one you carry.
**Reversed:** Isolation, withdrawal that has become hiding, a guidance refused.
**Persona:** The Hermit — a grey-robed elder alone on a snowy peak, a lantern holding a single six-pointed star raised before him, a staff in the other hand, contemplative and still.

## 10 — Wheel of Fortune
**Keywords:** cycles, fate, turning points, change
**Upright:** The wheel turns in your favour; a cycle completes and fortune shifts.
**Reversed:** A downturn, resisting an inevitable change, bad luck running its course.
**Persona:** Wheel of Fortune — a great golden wheel inscribed with mystic letters turning in the clouds, a sphinx above, a serpent descending, a jackal-headed figure rising, the four winged beasts reading in the corners.

## 11 — Justice
**Keywords:** fairness, truth, cause and effect, accountability
**Upright:** Truth weighed without flinching; you reap exactly what was sown.
**Reversed:** Unfairness, evasion, accounts left unsettled, dishonesty with oneself.
**Persona:** Justice — a crowned figure enthroned between two pillars, an upraised double-edged sword in one hand and balanced scales in the other, level-eyed and impartial.

## 12 — The Hanged Man
**Keywords:** surrender, new perspective, pause, letting go
**Upright:** Suspend the struggle; the answer comes when you see it upside down.
**Reversed:** Stalling, needless martyrdom, clinging when it is time to release.
**Persona:** The Hanged Man — a man suspended serenely by one ankle from a living T-shaped tree, hands behind his back, a calm radiant halo about his head, at peace in the reversal.

## 13 — Death
**Keywords:** endings, transformation, transition, release
**Upright:** A door closes so another can open; let the dead thing go and be remade.
**Reversed:** Resisting a necessary ending, clinging to what is already over.
**Persona:** Death — a skeletal rider in black armour on a pale white horse, a black banner bearing a white rose, a fallen king and a kneeling figure before him, a sun rising between two towers on the horizon.

## 14 — Temperance
**Keywords:** balance, moderation, patience, blending
**Upright:** Find the middle measure; patience and blending turn extremes into harmony.
**Reversed:** Imbalance, excess, impatience, oil and water refusing to mix.
**Persona:** Temperance — a winged angel with one foot on land and one in a stream, pouring water endlessly between two golden cups, a path leading to a crowned mountain behind, calm and exact.

## 15 — The Devil
**Keywords:** bondage, attachment, shadow, materialism
**Upright:** See the chain you could simply step out of; name the attachment that owns you.
**Reversed:** Breaking free, reclaiming power, releasing a craving's grip.
**Persona:** The Devil — a horned half-goat figure on a black pedestal, a torch held downward, two loosely chained human figures below who could lift the chains off at any moment, shadow lit by a low red glow.

## 16 — The Tower
**Keywords:** sudden upheaval, revelation, collapse, awakening
**Upright:** A flash of truth topples the false structure; what falls needed to fall.
**Reversed:** Disaster narrowly avoided, or change resisted until it forces its way through.
**Persona:** The Tower — a tall stone tower struck by a bolt of lightning, its golden crown blasted off, flames in the windows, two figures falling headlong against a black sky, sudden and total.

## 17 — The Star
**Keywords:** hope, renewal, inspiration, serenity
**Upright:** After the storm, calm and hope; you are guided, replenished, seen.
**Reversed:** Lost faith, discouragement, the light hard to feel through the clouds.
**Persona:** The Star — a kneeling nude figure by a still pool under one great eight-pointed star and seven smaller ones, pouring water from two jugs onto land and water, a bird in a tree behind, tranquil and luminous.

## 18 — The Moon
**Keywords:** illusion, intuition, the unconscious, the unclear
**Upright:** Not all is as it seems; let intuition lead through the half-light and unease.
**Reversed:** Confusion lifting, a fear seen for what it is, deception revealed.
**Persona:** The Moon — a pale full moon dripping light over a winding path between two towers, a dog and a wolf baying upward, a crayfish crawling from the water, silver-blue and uncertain.

## 19 — The Sun
**Keywords:** joy, success, vitality, clarity
**Upright:** Warmth, success, plain happiness; the thing is good and you may simply enjoy it.
**Reversed:** A passing cloud, joy dimmed or delayed, optimism that needs grounding.
**Persona:** The Sun — a radiant child crowned with a sunflower riding a white horse before a wall of tall sunflowers, a great beaming sun overhead, a red banner streaming, pure and bright.

## 20 — Judgement
**Keywords:** reckoning, awakening, rebirth, the call
**Upright:** A reckoning and a calling; rise, forgive the old self, answer the summons.
**Reversed:** Self-doubt, a call ignored, refusing to face the verdict.
**Persona:** Judgement — a great angel sounding a trumpet from the clouds, pale figures rising with arms uplifted from open graves below, a banner with a cross, snow-capped mountains behind, solemn and awe-struck.

## 21 — The World
**Keywords:** completion, fulfilment, wholeness, arrival
**Upright:** The cycle closes whole; you have arrived, integrated, complete.
**Reversed:** So close yet unfinished, a loose end, completion just out of reach.
**Persona:** The World — a dancing figure draped in a violet sash within a great laurel wreath, a wand in each hand, the four winged beasts (man, eagle, bull, lion) in the corners, cosmic and triumphant.
